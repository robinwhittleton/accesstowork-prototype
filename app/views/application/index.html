1. Overview

You can apply for an Access to Work grant if you have a disability or health condition that makes it difficult for you to do your job.

The grant can pay for special equipment or support to help you:

  • do work tasks
  • get to and from work

You must:

  • have a paid job or be self-employed (you can’t get a grant for voluntary work)
  • have an interview and need help communicating

You can't get a grant to help with business start-up costs.

The grant doesn’t have to be paid back.

Your condition

Other benefits

You might not get a grant if you already get any of:

  • Incapacity Benefit
  • Job Seekers Allowance
  • Employment and Support Allowance
  • Severe Disability Allowance
  • Income Support
  • National Insurance Credits

If you already have a grant

If you have special equipment that's been paid for with an Access to Work grant you may be able to take it with you if you change jobs.

You'll have to apply for a new Access to Work grant if your grant pays for travel costs and you change where you work or live.
